# Product North Star

## The job to be done

A person is writing in Notes, Mail, Slack, a browser, or an editor. They do
not want to open an AI tool, select a microphone, configure a model, or manage
a transcript. They want to hold one key, say what they mean, release it, and
continue working.

VachaVox makes that feel private, fast, and calming.

## Customer promise

> Hold a key. Speak. VachaVox types polished text anywhere on your Mac.

Supporting promises:
- Your voice stays on your Mac.
- It works offline after setup.
- Pay once. No subscription. No account.
- It uses the best local speech capability available for your Mac without
  making you learn what a model is.

## Buyer

Primary:
- ordinary Mac users who write messages, notes, emails, documents, tickets,
  and prompts;
- people who dislike subscriptions;
- people who value privacy;
- people who have not installed developer tooling or local models.

Secondary:
- developers, writers, students, knowledge workers, accessibility-minded users,
  and non-native speakers.

Not primary:
- transcription professionals needing batch workflows;
- meeting recording customers;
- AI power users seeking many model settings;
- teams requiring shared admin/data infrastructure.

## Product framing

Use:
- “Private dictation for Mac.”
- “Works offline after setup.”
- “One-time purchase.”
- “Hold a key. Speak. Type anywhere.”

Avoid:
- “AI platform.”
- “Agent.”
- “LLM.”
- “Whisper / Parakeet / Core ML” in normal UI.
- unsupported claims such as “most accurate,” “better than Apple,” “perfect,”
  or “works in every field.”

## The $0.99 rule

Every feature must answer one question:

> Does this make first-time dictation feel more obvious, more reliable, or more
> private for a person who paid one dollar?

If not, it is out of V1.

## What V1 is

- menu-bar dictation utility;
- one default shortcut plus a simple change control;
- on-device/system-local speech asset preparation;
- a tiny visible listening/transcribing confirmation;
- polished-text toggle on by default;
- paste into the focused app when Apple-supported permission flow permits;
- reliable copy fallback;
- launch-at-login and listening sound optional toggles;
- simple repair state if local speech is unavailable.

## What V1 is not

- a chatbot;
- an audio-file transcriber;
- a meeting recorder;
- a model browser;
- a writing app;
- a voice clone tool;
- a browser dashboard;
- a cloud service;
- an iOS app;
- a subscription upsell funnel.

## Future only after V1 earns the right

- additional offline languages once tested;
- optional audio-file transcription;
- optional advanced mode;
- one paid “Pro” upgrade only if there is a real, durable customer benefit.
